Linus Torvalds

The creator of Linux

Biography

Linus Benedict Torvalds is a Finnish software engineer who is the creator and, historically, the lead developer of the Linux kernel, used by Linux distributions and other operating systems such as Android. He also created the distributed version control system Git.

Achievements

  • 1991 - Created Linux kernel and version control system Git
  • 2004 - Named one of the "100 most influential people in the world" by Time magazine
  • 2005 - Torvalds received the Vollum Award from Reed College
  • 2008 - He was inducted into the Hall of Fellows of the Computer History Museum
  • 2010 - He was awarded the C&C Prize by the NEC Corporation
  • 2012 - Torvalds was one of the inaugural inductees into the Internet Hall of Fame
  • 2014 - the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers named Torvalds as the 2014 recipient of the IEEE Computer Society's Computer Pioneer Award
  • 2018 -2018 Ibuka award was conferred to Linus Torvalds "For his leadership of the development and proliferation of Linux.
